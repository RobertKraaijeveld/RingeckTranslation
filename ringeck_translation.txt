This text is about many good general lessons of the long sword
---------------------------------------------------------------
If you want to display art,
Step with the left (foot) (to) (his) right with strikes,
and to (the) left with (strikes to his) right,
so you will fight strongly.

Commentary: 
This is the first lesson of the long sword: (Namely) that you should learn to perform the strikes correctly
from both sides. If you want to fight strongly and righteously, then note this:  

When you want to strike from the right side, then put your left foot in front, and when you want to
strike from the left side, then put your right foot in front. If you then strike the oberhaw from the right,
then follow the strike with your right foot; if you don't do that, your strike will be incorrect and crooked, 
because your right side stays behind. Because of that, the strike is too short, and it cannot follow 
it's correct trajectory: downwards and to the other side, in front of the left foot. 

The same applies when you strike from the left side and do not follow the strike with your left foot: 
Then (that/the) strike is incorrect as well. 

Therefore, (pay attention to/note) from which side you strike: Follow the strike with that same foot, 
so that you can execute all of your techniques correctly, and with strength.

And like this, all the other strikes will be struck (as well).
